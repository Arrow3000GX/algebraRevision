GCSE Algebra Revision

Key Concepts

  • Variables: Symbols used to represent numbers (e.g., x, y).
  • Expressions: Combinations of variables and numbers (e.g., 2x + 3).
  • Equations: Statements that two expressions are equal (e.g., 2x + 3 = 7).
  • Solving Equations: Finding the value of the variable that makes the equation true.
  • Factoring: Rewriting an expression as a product of its factors (e.g., x² - 9 = (x - 3)(x + 3)).

Example Problems

Example 1: Solving a Linear Equation

Solve for x: 2x + 5 = 13

Solution: Subtract 5 from both sides: 2x = 8. Then divide by 2: x = 4.

Example 2: Factoring a Quadratic

Factor the expression: x² + 5x + 6

Solution: (x + 2)(x + 3)
